Passportable
A map-based, drag-and-drop travel planning platform

The Easiest Way to Plan a Trip

I love travelling. Whether I was travelling with friends, family, or by myself the workflow seemed the same: use Google Maps for research, then switch to Google Docs or Microsoft Word to create the itinerary. While building an itinerary in a text editor is simple, lightweight, flexible, customizeable, and easily shareable, it's also prone to typos and errors. And if plans change that affect the timing of other events, it's a hassle to have to go through the entire itinerary to make the necessary changes.

Passportable is a travel planning platform that makes it easy for anyone to create and share itineraries. Places and activities become "cards" which users can add to their itinerary and shuffle around. Users can also edit their cards, specifying how long they'll be staying at a location for. Passportable automatically updates the itinerary with transportation information and suggests leave times in order to stay on track. with a clean, responsive user interface inspired by retro travel postcards. If you're going on a trip with friends, you'll have the option of sharing your itinerary with them so that everyone is on the same page. Once you're ready to embark on your adventure, Passportable compiles a print-ready PDF.

Technology

Under the hood, Passportable is a simple web app with a frontend built with HTML5, CSS3, and Javascript (with some JQuery and Bootstrap) and a Firebase backend. It relies on the Google Maps and Mapbox APIs for its navigation features.


Commercialization

While a version of this platform will be made freely available to anyone who wishes to use it, it will generate revenue with ''freemium'' pricing.
